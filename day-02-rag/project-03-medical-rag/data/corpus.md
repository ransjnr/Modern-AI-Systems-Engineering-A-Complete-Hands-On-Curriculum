# Sample medical reference corpus (educational only)

## Hypertension
Blood pressure is the force of blood against artery walls. Hypertension means
consistently high blood pressure. Lifestyle measures that help include reducing
salt intake, regular aerobic exercise, maintaining a healthy weight, limiting
alcohol, and not smoking. Persistent high readings should be assessed by a clinician.

## Type 2 Diabetes
Type 2 diabetes is a condition where the body becomes resistant to insulin or does
not produce enough. Management includes a balanced diet, regular physical activity,
weight management, and medication when prescribed. Red-flag symptoms include
excessive thirst, frequent urination, and unexplained weight loss.

## Common Cold vs Influenza
A common cold develops gradually with a runny nose and mild symptoms. Influenza
tends to start suddenly with fever, body aches, and fatigue. Rest, fluids, and
time are the mainstays; seek care for difficulty breathing or a very high fever.

## Hydration
Adequate hydration supports circulation, temperature regulation, and kidney
function. Needs vary by activity and climate. Dark urine and persistent thirst
can indicate dehydration.
